Kathak

History

The roots of this dance form trace back to Sanskrit Hindu text on performing arts called ‘Natya Shastra’ written by ancient Indian theatrologist and musicologist Bharata Muni. It is presumed that the first complete version of the text was completed between 200 BCE to 200 CE, but some sources mention the timeframe to be around 500 BCE and 500 CE.

Origin

Kathak is native to north india it started from 4th century bc by telling tales of Mahabharata and Ramayana in temples.


Significance

Kathak has emerged as a distinct dance form. Being the only classical dance of India having links with Muslim culture, it represents a unique synthesis of Hindu and Muslim genius in art.

Costume

The Kathak costumes were initially ghaghra (long skirt), Choli (Blouse) and a veil for women. For the men, the costume is the Dhoti.

Resonating element of Kathak

Ghoomar dance resonates with kathak. People perform ghoomar dance on festivals and ghoomar dance is very much polpular in Haryana and Rajasthan
